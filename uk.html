Study in the United Kingdom

Globally respected degrees, shorter courses, and a 2-year Graduate Route work visa

Home / Destinations / UK

Why Study in the UK?

The UK is home to historic, world-leading universities and offers some of the shortest degree durations globally — most master's programs take just one year, saving you time and money. After graduating, the Graduate Route visa lets you stay and work for 2 years (3 years for PhD graduates).

📝
Visa Type
Student Route Visa
🎓
Main Intakes
September & January
⏱️
Processing Time
3–8 weeks
💼
Work Rights
20 hrs/week + Graduate Route

Required Documents

  • Valid passport
  • Academic transcripts & certificates
  • IELTS / IELTS UKVI / PTE / approved English test
  • Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) from the university
  • Statement of Purpose / Personal Statement
  • Letters of Recommendation
  • CV/Resume (for some programs)
  • Proof of funds (28-day bank statement rule)
  • Tuberculosis (TB) test certificate
  • Academic Technology Approval Scheme (ATAS) certificate — for certain subjects
  • Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS) payment

Admission Requirements

  • HSC for undergraduate; Bachelor's for postgraduate
  • IELTS typically 6.0–7.0 (varies by course)
  • Relevant academic background for the chosen course
  • Strong personal statement
  • Funds held for at least 28 consecutive days
💡 Tip: The UK uses UCAS for undergraduate applications, with an earlier deadline (often January). Postgraduate applications are usually direct to the university and more flexible.
💡 Tip: US applications are holistic — essays, extracurriculars, and recommendations matter as much as grades. Start 9–12 months before your intake.

Step-by-Step Process & Timeline

12 months before

Profile Assessment & Test Prep

Shortlist universities and prepare for TOEFL/IELTS and SAT/GRE/GMAT.

8–10 months before

Application Submission

Submit applications with SOP, LORs, transcripts, and test scores.

5–7 months before

Admission & I-20

Receive admission, accept the offer, and the university issues your Form I-20.

3–4 months before

SEVIS Fee & DS-160

Pay the SEVIS (I-901) fee and complete the DS-160 visa application.

2–3 months before

Visa Interview

Attend the F-1 interview at the US Embassy in Dhaka with all documents.

Departure

Fly to the USA

Pre-departure briefing, accommodation, and you can enter up to 30 days before your program start.
