Study in Canada

High-quality education, PR-friendly pathways, and a Post-Graduation Work Permit

Home / Destinations / Canada

Why Study in Canada?

Canada offers globally recognized degrees, a welcoming multicultural society, and clear pathways from study to permanent residence. Eligible graduates can apply for the Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) for up to 3 years, gaining Canadian work experience that supports PR applications.

📝
Visa Type
Study Permit
🎓
Main Intakes
Fall (Sep), Winter (Jan), Summer (May)
⏱️
Processing Time
4–12 weeks
💼
Work Rights
Part-time + PGWP up to 3 yrs

Required Documents

  • Valid passport
  • Academic transcripts & certificates
  • IELTS / PTE / TOEFL scores
  • Letter of Acceptance from a Designated Learning Institution (DLI)
  • Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL/TAL), where required
  • Proof of funds / GIC (Guaranteed Investment Certificate)
  • Statement of Purpose / study plan
  • Tuition fee payment receipt
  • Medical examination (panel physician)
  • Biometrics & digital photo
  • Police clearance certificate (if required)

Admission Requirements

  • HSC for undergraduate; Bachelor's for postgraduate
  • IELTS 6.0–6.5 (often via SDS stream)
  • Acceptance from a Designated Learning Institution (DLI)
  • Proof of funds covering tuition + living
  • Genuine intent and clear study plan
💡 Tip: Many students apply via the Student Direct Stream (SDS) for faster processing, which typically requires a GIC and upfront tuition payment. A Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL) is now required for most applicants — we handle this for you.

Step-by-Step Process & Timeline

8–10 months before

2–4 months before

Departure

Collect your study permit at the port of entry on arrival.
